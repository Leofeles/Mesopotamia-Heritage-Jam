Mesopotamian Gods


What This Is All About

Mesopotamia was a civilization that stood in the mid east for millennia. Its rise and fall took thousands of years and over that time human civilization transformed. Mesopotamia is often referred to as the cradle of civilization. Marking a time in human history when written language begun to emerge, complex systems of trade and agriculture were refined. Technological advancements allowed for cities to grow and specialize.

Mesopotamia has the first rerecorded religion. This civilization had a polytheistic approach to religion, with thousands of gods in its pantheon. Many gods as well have multiple names because of the two linguistic groups who dwelled in ancient Mesopotamia in the third millennium BC: the Sumerian speakers and the Akkadian speakers. Mesopotamian conceptions of the gods in relation to humans took place on three levels of existence.
